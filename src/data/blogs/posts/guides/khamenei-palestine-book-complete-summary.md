# Palestine: The Complete Summary of Khamenei's Book on the Palestinian Cause

Among the many books authored by Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, his writings on Palestine stand as perhaps his most important contribution to Islamic thought on resistance and liberation. His book on Palestine—compiled from decades of speeches, statements, and writings—provides a comprehensive analysis of the Palestinian cause, the nature of the Zionist entity, and the duty of Muslims to support liberation.

This is a complete summary of Khamenei's Palestine book, capturing every essential point he made about this central issue.

---

## The Historical Foundation: How Palestine Was Stolen

### The Colonial Nature of Zionism

Khamenei begins his analysis by establishing the colonial nature of the Zionist project:

"Zionism was not a movement of indigenous people returning to their homeland. It was a European colonial project from its inception. Theodor Herzl and the founders of Zionism were European Jews who had no connection to Palestine. They chose Palestine not because of historical ties, but because it served the colonial interests of European powers."

He documents:
- Zionism emerged in the late 19th century alongside European colonialism
- Zionist leaders negotiated with various colonial powers (Britain, Germany, even Ottoman Empire)
- The goal was always to establish a Western-aligned state in the Middle East
- Indigenous Palestinians were always intended to be removed

### Britain's Criminal Role

Khamenei extensively documents Britain's role in creating Israel:

"The Balfour Declaration of 1917 was one of the greatest crimes of colonialism. Britain promised land it did not own to people who did not live there, without consulting the indigenous population. This was not a legitimate political act—it was theft authorized by a colonial power."

Key points:
- Britain promised Palestine to Zionists while Palestinians constituted 90% of the population
- The British Mandate facilitated Zionist immigration and land acquisition
- Britain suppressed Palestinian resistance while enabling Zionist colonization
- The entire foundation of Israel is based on British colonial authorization

### The 1948 Ethnic Cleansing

Khamenei provides detailed analysis of the Nakba:

"1948 was not a war of independence—it was a premeditated campaign of ethnic cleansing. Zionist forces implemented Plan Dalet to systematically empty Palestine of its Arab population. Over 750,000 Palestinians were expelled, over 500 villages were destroyed, and those who fled were never allowed to return."

He documents specific massacres:
- Deir Yassin: Over 100 civilians killed, including women and children
- Tantura: Mass killings of Palestinian men
- Lydda and Ramle: Forced expulsion of 50,000+ Palestinians

The Nakba was not accidental—it was the intentional creation of a Jewish state through the removal of the Arab majority.

## The Nature of the Zionist Entity

### Israel Is Not a Legitimate State

Khamenei's central thesis is that Israel lacks legitimacy:

"Israel is not a legitimate nation-state. It is a colonial project established through ethnic cleansing, maintained through apartheid, and sustained by Western support. No amount of recognition by Western powers can confer legitimacy on a project built on stolen land and corpses."

He argues legitimacy requires:
- Consensus of the indigenous population (Israel never had this)
- Acquisition of territory through legal means (Israel acquired through force)
- Treatment of all citizens equally (Israel practices apartheid)
- Respect for international law (Israel violates it consistently)

Israel fails every test of legitimacy.

### The Apartheid Character

Khamenei extensively documents Israel's apartheid nature:

"Israel is an apartheid state by any definition of international law. Different laws apply to different ethnic groups. Palestinians in the West Bank live under military occupation while Israeli settlers live under civil law. Palestinian citizens of Israel face systematic discrimination. This is not democracy—it is ethnocracy."

Examples provided:
- Palestinians cannot travel freely in their own land
- Palestinians face restrictions on building and residency
- Palestinians are subject to military courts while Jews face civil courts
- Palestinians are denied the right of return while any Jew can immigrate

### The Expansionist Agenda

Khamenei warns against viewing Israel as static:

"Israel has never defined its borders because its agenda is expansion. From 1948 to 1967 to today, Israel has continuously expanded through settlement and war. The goal is 'Greater Israel'—and this threatens not just Palestinians but all neighbors."

He notes:
- Settlements continuously expand in the West Bank
- Israel has attacked every neighbor (Egypt, Jordan, Syria, Lebanon)
- Israeli politicians regularly call for expansion
- The agenda has never changed—only the methods

## The Duty of Muslims Toward Palestine

### Palestine Is an Islamic Issue

Khamenei argues that Palestine is not merely a Palestinian or Arab issue:

"Palestine is an Islamic issue. Jerusalem is holy to all Muslims. The occupation of Muslim land is the concern of all Muslims. The suffering of Palestinian Muslims—and Palestinian Christians—is the responsibility of the entire Ummah."

He cites Islamic principles:
- Muslims are one body; when one part suffers, all suffer
- Jerusalem (Al-Quds) is the first Qibla and third holiest site
- Defense of oppressed Muslims is a religious obligation
- Abandoning Palestine is abandoning a core Islamic duty

### The Duty of Support

Khamenei outlines the duty of Muslims to support Palestine:

"Support for Palestine is not optional charity—it is mandatory obligation. Every Muslim who has the ability must contribute to the liberation of Palestine according to their capacity. This includes financial support, political support, and if necessary, physical support."

Levels of duty:
- **Individual duty:** Speaking truth about Palestine, boycotting Israel
- **Collective duty:** Governments providing material support to resistance
- **Emergency duty:** Military support when Muslims are under attack

### The Prohibition of Normalization

Khamenei strongly condemns normalization with Israel:

"Normalization with Israel is haram—forbidden. It legitimizes occupation, betrays the Palestinian people, and weakens the resistance. Any Muslim government that normalizes with Israel has betrayed the Ummah and will answer to God for this treason."

He argues normalization:
- Legitimizes the theft of Palestinian land
- Undermines the resistance
- Serves Western interests, not Muslim interests
- Violates the rights of Palestinian refugees

## The Solution: Resistance, Not Negotiation

### The Failure of Negotiations

Khamenei provides extensive analysis of why negotiations have failed:

"The so-called peace process has been a disaster for Palestinians. Decades of negotiations have produced only more settlements, more occupation, and more suffering. The reason is simple: Israel does not want peace—it wants land without people."

Why negotiations fail:
- Israel negotiates to buy time for settlement expansion
- Western mediators are biased toward Israel
- The framework assumes Israel's legitimacy
- Palestinians are forced to negotiate with their occupier
- The "two-state solution" has always been a mirage

### The Only Path: Resistance

Khamenei argues that resistance is the only viable path:

"Liberation has never been achieved through negotiations with the occupier. Every liberation struggle in history—from Algeria to South Africa to Vietnam—succeeded through resistance. Palestine will be liberated through resistance, not through conferences with the occupier."

He defines resistance as:
- Military resistance against occupation forces
- Political resistance against normalization
- Economic resistance through boycott
- Cultural resistance against erasure
- Intellectual resistance against propaganda

### The Resistance Model: Hezbollah

Khamenei points to Hezbollah's success as evidence:

"Hezbollah demonstrated that resistance works. Israel occupied southern Lebanon for 22 years. Diplomacy achieved nothing. The United Nations passed resolutions that Israel ignored. Only resistance forced Israel to withdraw in 2000."

Lessons from Hezbollah:
- Israel understands only the language of force
- Resistance can defeat a superior military
- Patience and persistence are essential
- The resistance has popular support
- International law serves the powerful, not the just

## The Role of the International Community

### Western Complicity

Khamenei extensively documents Western support for Israel:

"Western support for Israel is not accidental or incidental—it is fundamental. Israel is a Western colonial project that serves Western interests. The United States, Britain, France, Germany, and other Western powers provide the weapons, the diplomatic cover, and the financial support that enables Israeli crimes."

Western support includes:
- Billions in annual military aid to Israel
- Veto of UN resolutions critical of Israel
- Diplomatic protection for Israeli officials
- Intelligence sharing and military cooperation
- Media propaganda supporting Israel

### The Hypocrisy of Human Rights

Khamenei exposes Western double standards:

"Western governments lecture the world about human rights while supporting a state that has violated every human rights principle. They impose sanctions on Iran for its nuclear program while ignoring Israel's nuclear weapons. They condemn Palestinian resistance while arming Israeli aggression."

Double standards documented:
- Sanctions for Iran, aid for Israel
- Condemnation for resistance, support for occupation
- Nuclear concerns for Iran, silence on Israel's arsenal
- Human rights for some, not for Palestinians

### The Role of the United Nations

Khamenei critiques the UN's failure:

"The United Nations has failed Palestine completely. Resolution after resolution has been ignored by Israel with no consequence. The Security Council is paralyzed by American vetoes. The international system is designed to serve powerful states, not to deliver justice."

UN failures:
- Partition resolution was imposed without Palestinian consent
- Resolutions demanding Israeli withdrawal have been ignored
- The right of return has never been implemented
- Israel has never faced consequences for violations

## The Future: Liberation Is Certain

### The Inevitability of Liberation

Khamenei expresses certainty about Palestine's liberation:

"Palestine will be liberated. This is not wishful thinking—it is historical truth. No colonial project has survived indefinitely. The apartheid regime in South Africa fell. The Soviet Union collapsed. The colonial empires dissolved. Israel will meet the same fate."

Reasons for certainty:
- Colonial projects always eventually fail
- Israel's demographic reality is unsustainable
- The resistance continues and grows
- Global opinion is shifting against Israel
- Divine promise of victory for the oppressed

### The Islamic Dimension

Khamenei emphasizes the spiritual dimension:

"The liberation of Palestine is not merely a political goal—it is a spiritual duty. Muslims believe in the promise of God that the oppressed will be victorious. The Quran tells us that the oppressed will inherit the earth. This promise applies to Palestine."

Islamic basis for hope:
- God's promise to the oppressed
- The prophetic tradition of liberation
- The spiritual significance of Jerusalem
- The martyrs who have sacrificed
- The prayers of millions

### The Role of the New Generation

Khamenei places hope in the youth:

"The older generation of Muslim leaders has largely failed Palestine. But the new generation is different. They are not bound by the compromises of their predecessors. They see the truth clearly and are willing to act. The liberation of Palestine will come through this new generation."

Signs of hope:
- Youth-led resistance movements
- Global awareness through social media
- Rejection of normalization by young Muslims
- Continued commitment to the cause
