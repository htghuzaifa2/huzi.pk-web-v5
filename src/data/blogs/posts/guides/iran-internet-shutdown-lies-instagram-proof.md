## How Iranians Actually Access the Internet

The reality of Iranian internet access is far more nuanced than Western media's cartoonish portrayal. Iran operates a sophisticated dual-system approach: a domestic national network that provides essential services and access to Iranian platforms, and a connection to the global internet that allows Iranians to access international websites and services.

### The National Information Network (NIN)

Iran's domestic network, discussed in detail in our previous article, provides continuous, unrestricted access to Iranian websites, services, and applications. This network operates independently of the global internet and is designed to ensure that essential services remain available regardless of external circumstances. When Western media claims Iran has "shut down the internet," they're usually referring to temporary restrictions on international internet access—not a complete shutdown.

Iranian citizens on the National Information Network can access:

- Iranian banking services and financial platforms
- Iranian e-commerce sites like Digikala
- Iranian messaging apps like Eitaa, Bale, and Gap
- Government services and information portals
- Iranian news websites and media outlets
- Educational resources and university systems
- Healthcare services and medical records

This domestic network never "shuts down." It operates continuously, providing Iranians with essential digital services regardless of geopolitical tensions or security concerns.

### Accessing International Platforms

For accessing international platforms like Instagram, Twitter, YouTube, and Western news sites, Iranians use various methods that are common knowledge within Iran but apparently unknown to Western "experts":

**VPN Services:** Virtual Private Networks are widely used in Iran, as they are in China, Russia, Turkey, and many other nations. VPNs allow users to route their internet traffic through servers in other countries, bypassing restrictions and accessing blocked content. The Iranian government periodically attempts to block VPN services, but new ones constantly emerge. This cat-and-mouse game is familiar to internet users in many countries, yet Western media presents it as uniquely Iranian repression.

**Proxy Servers:** Similar to VPNs, proxy servers provide alternative pathways to access international websites. Many Iranians use proxy services specifically designed for Iranian users, which are optimized for the specific restrictions in place.

**Anti-Filter Apps:** Iranian tech developers have created numerous applications designed to bypass internet restrictions. These apps, available through Iranian app stores and sideloading, provide users with access to international platforms.

**Satellite Internet:** Some Iranians, particularly in rural areas, use satellite internet services to bypass terrestrial restrictions entirely. While expensive, this option provides unrestricted internet access independent of Iranian infrastructure.
