# Khamenei's Final Message to the Muslim Ummah: Unity Against Western Oppression

Throughout his decades of leadership, Ayatollah Ali Khamenei delivered a consistent message to Muslims worldwide: Unite against Western oppression. Support Palestine. Reject sectarian division. Trust in God. Stand firm against intimidation. His message was not merely political—it was a call to return to Islamic principles of unity, justice, and resistance.

As we honor the Shaheed, his message to the Muslim Ummah becomes more important than ever. This is a message that will outlive all of us—a call to action that must not be ignored.

---

## The Call for Muslim Unity

### The Greatest Weakness: Division

Khamenei repeatedly warned that division among Muslims was their greatest weakness:

"The enemies of Islam have always tried to create division among Muslims. They know that a united Muslim Ummah is an unstoppable force. They know that divided, Muslims can be conquered one by one."

The evidence is clear:
- Sunni-Shia conflicts serve Western interests
- Arab-Persian tensions benefit Israel
- National divisions prevent Muslim cooperation
- Sectarian violence weakens the entire Ummah

Khamenei understood that these divisions were not natural—they were cultivated by Western intelligence agencies and regional dictators to prevent Muslim unity.

### Beyond Sectarian Lines

Despite being a Shia scholar, Khamenei consistently reached beyond sectarian lines:
- He praised Sunni scholars and movements
- He called Sunni and Shia "brothers in Islam"
- He opposed sectarian violence from any source
- He supported Sunni resistance movements

His message was clear: The division between Sunni and Shia is less important than the division between those who serve Western interests and those who serve Muslim interests.

### The Enemy of My Enemy

Khamenei's approach to unity was pragmatic:
- Iran supported Sunni Hamas in Palestine
- Iran supported Sunni movements in resistance
- Iran welcomed cooperation with any Muslim nation
- Iran opposed any Muslim government serving Western interests

The criterion was not sect—it was position on Western imperialism and Zionism.

## Palestine: The Central Cause

### The Key to Muslim Dignity

Khamenei consistently identified Palestine as the central issue for the Muslim Ummah:

"Palestine is not just a Palestinian issue—it is an Islamic issue, a human issue. Every Muslim has a duty to support Palestine. Every Muslim has a duty to oppose Zionism."

His reasoning was clear:
- Jerusalem is holy to all Muslims
- Palestinians are occupied in their own land
- Israel is a Western colonial project
- Abandoning Palestine means abandoning Muslim dignity

### The Test of Sincerity

Khamenei used Palestine as a test of sincerity:
- Governments that normalize with Israel serve Western interests
- Leaders who abandon Palestine are traitors to the Ummah
- Those who speak for Palestine but do nothing are hypocrites
- Only those who actively support resistance are sincere

This test exposed many Muslim governments that talked about Palestine while serving Western interests.

### The Solution: Resistance, Not Negotiation

Khamenei rejected the "peace process" as a fraud:
- Negotiations with Israel legitimize occupation
- The "two-state solution" is a delay tactic
- Only resistance can achieve liberation
- Only return of all Palestinian land is acceptable

His position was clear: Israel must be dismantled as a colonial entity, and Palestine must be liberated from the river to the sea.

## Rejecting Western Domination

### The Illusion of Western Friendship

Khamenei warned Muslim nations against seeking Western friendship:

"Western powers do not have friends—they have interests. When your interests align with theirs, they call you 'friend.' When your interests diverge, they destroy you."

The evidence supports his warning:
- Saddam Hussein was a Western ally before he was an enemy
- Gaddafi cooperated with the West before being overthrown
- The Shah of Iran was a Western ally until his people overthrew him
- Hosni Mubarak was a Western ally until he became inconvenient

Western "friendship" is conditional on servitude. When you stop serving their interests, you become an enemy.

### The True Path: Independence

Khamenei called Muslim nations to independence:
- Develop indigenous capabilities
- Build cooperation among Muslim nations
- Reject Western military bases on Muslim land
- Use Muslim resources for Muslim benefit
- Let Muslim nations determine their own future

This path is difficult—Western powers punish those who take it. But it is the only path to dignity.

### The Example of Iran

Iran under Khamenei demonstrated that independence is possible:
- Iran survived Western sanctions
- Iran developed despite Western restrictions
- Iran maintained its sovereignty
- Iran supported the oppressed despite Western pressure

If Iran can do it, other Muslim nations can do it. The only obstacle is the will to resist.

## Standing Against Zionist Crimes

### Israel as the Common Enemy

Khamenei identified Israel as the common enemy of all Muslims:

"Israel is a cancerous tumor in the heart of the Muslim world. It was planted by Western powers to divide and weaken Muslims. It will be removed—this is a promise."

His reasoning:
- Israel occupies Muslim land
- Israel kills Muslims with impunity
- Israel threatens all Muslim nations
- Israel is the base for Western operations in the region

No Muslim nation is safe while Israel exists. Therefore, all Muslims have an interest in opposing Israel.

### The Normalization Trap

Khamenei strongly opposed normalization with Israel:
- Normalization betrays Palestine
- Normalization legitimizes occupation
- Normalization strengthens Israel
- Normalization serves Western interests

He called normalization "treason against the Palestinian cause" and "a stab in the back of the resistance."

### The Resistance Axis

Khamenei championed the "Axis of Resistance":
- Iran
- Hezbollah in Lebanon
- Hamas and Islamic Jihad in Palestine
- Resistance forces in Iraq
- Houthis in Yemen

This axis demonstrated that resistance is possible. It is not as powerful as Western-backed forces, but it has the moral clarity and determination that Western-backed regimes lack.

## Trust in God, Not in Power

### The Source of Strength

Khamenei's message always returned to spiritual foundations:

"Do not fear their numbers or their weapons. Trust in God. Those who trust in God cannot be defeated by those who trust only in power."

This was not mere rhetoric—it was the foundation of his own resistance:
- He survived assassination attempts through God's protection
- He resisted Western pressure through faith
- He maintained hope through trust in God's justice
- He accepted hardship as a test from God

### The Material vs. The Spiritual

Western civilization is materialistic—it measures success in wealth, weapons, and power. Khamenei offered a different measure:
- Success is fidelity to truth
- Success is standing for justice
- Success is serving the oppressed
- Success is trust in God

By material measures, Iran is weak compared to the West. By spiritual measures, Iran's resistance is a success that Western materialism cannot understand.

### The Ultimate Victory

Khamenei taught that the ultimate victory belongs to truth:

"Truth will prevail over falsehood. This is God's promise. It may take time, there may be difficulties, but the outcome is certain. Do not lose hope."

This is the message that sustained him through decades of pressure. It is the message that will sustain Muslims who continue his work.

## The Responsibility of Muslim Youth

### The Future Belongs to the Youth

Khamenei consistently addressed Muslim youth:

"The future belongs to you. Do not let the older generation's failures become your failures. Do not accept the world as it is—work to create the world as it should be."

He called on youth to:
- Educate themselves about their religion and history
- Reject Western cultural domination
- Build capabilities to serve their nations
- Maintain hope and resist despair
- Continue the struggle for justice

### Resist Despair

Khamenei warned against despair:

"The enemies want you to despair. They want you to believe that resistance is futile, that Western domination is permanent, that injustice cannot be defeated. Do not believe them. Despair is a weapon of the enemy."

His life was proof that despair is unjustified:
- The Shah seemed invincible—he was overthrown
- Israel seemed invincible—it was expelled from Lebanon
- Western power seemed absolute—it is now in decline

The future is not determined by current power relationships. It is determined by those who struggle.

### Build the Future

Khamenei called on youth to build:

"Do not just resist—build. Build knowledge, build capabilities, build institutions, build cooperation. The future will belong to those who build."

Resistance alone is not enough. Muslims must build alternatives to Western-dominated systems:
- Alternative media
- Alternative education
- Alternative economics
- Alternative technology

Khamenei's Iran attempted this, and while imperfect, it demonstrated that alternatives are possible.

## The Final Test: Continuing the Mission

### Khamenei's Death Is Not the End

Khamenei's message to the Ummah was not about himself:

"This movement is not about one person. It is about principles that will continue regardless of who leads. I am just a servant of this cause. If I am gone, others will continue."

His death does not end the mission. It tests whether Muslims will continue without him.

### The Choice Before Muslims

Every Muslim now faces a choice:
- Continue the resistance that Khamenei championed
- Return to accommodation with Western domination
- Abandon Palestine for practical benefits
- Accept division and weakness

Khamenei's message calls Muslims to choose resistance, unity, and faith.

### Honoring the Shaheed

The best way to honor a Shaheed is to continue their mission:
- Support Palestine as he supported Palestine
- Resist Western domination as he resisted
- Unite Muslims as he called for unity
- Trust in God as he trusted

Khamenei's physical presence is gone, but his message lives. It is now our responsibility to carry it forward.
